Why the code matters

One 8-digit code drives six outcomes

Classification isn't paperwork — it's the single decision your entire import or export economics hangs on. Get it wrong in either direction and it costs money: overpay silently, quarter after quarter, or underpay and answer for it later, with interest, up to five years on.

01

Import duty stack

BCD, Social Welfare Surcharge, AIDC and Health Cess — every rate keys off the 8-digit line. The wrong line means the wrong landed cost.

02

GST rate & invoice HSN

The HSN you print on every invoice, and the GST slab you charge, both flow from the same classification.

03

Licences & clearances

BIS, FSSAI, CDSCO, WPC, Legal Metrology, EPR — whether a clearance is triggered at all depends on where the product sits in the tariff.

04

Export incentives

RoDTEP, RoSCTL and drawback are all published against tariff lines. Mis-classify and you leave incentive money on the table.

05

FTA eligibility

Preferential rates under ASEAN, UAE CEPA, UK CETA and more are granted per tariff line — subject to rules of origin and CAROTAR 2020.

06

Penalty exposure

Under-declaration is recoverable for up to five years, with interest and penalty. The classification is the first thing an auditor checks.

Why now — 2025-26

Classification is now a monitoring problem, not a one-time opinion

India's tariff and exemption architecture has been rebuilt in the space of a year, and more is scheduled. A rate confirmed last quarter can be wrong this quarter. An opinion filed and forgotten is a liability waiting to surface.

That's why we don't sell a one-off PDF. We classify, then we watch — matching every change to your catalogue and re-doing the arithmetic before it hits your bill of entry.

Binding classification certainty in India is available only through a CAAR advance ruling, which is valid for five years (extendable). The more duty-determinative detail, the tighter the code: material and composition percentages, the product's principal function, how it's sold (retail set or bulk), power source, and target user. For textiles, knit-vs-woven and fibre split matter; for footwear, upper and sole material; for supplements, whether therapeutic claims are made. A product image and a product page URL (as reference text) help too.
